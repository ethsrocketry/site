# Join Us Page Content

## Page Header
**eyebrow:** BUILD WITH US
**title:** Join ETHS Rocketry
**subtitle:** ETHS Rocketry is where motivated students turn curiosity into real engineering. You will design, build, test, and improve systems that have to work in the real world — while learning with a team that takes mentorship, safety, and iteration seriously.

## Section 1: What you will learn by doing
**heading:** What you will learn by doing

**skills:**
- **title:** Rocketry fundamentals
  **description:** Learn stability, propulsion basics, recovery systems, avionics integration, launch operations, and post-flight analysis through real team projects.

- **title:** Software + technical workflow
  **description:** Build practical software habits with version control, debugging, data handling, and lightweight automation used in engineering projects.

- **title:** CAD and hardware design
  **description:** Model and revise components in Autodesk Inventor and Onshape, then connect digital designs to fabrication and assembly constraints.

- **title:** Simulation + analysis
  **description:** Use OpenRocket and CFD-style analysis to estimate performance, compare design options, and make better design decisions before building.

- **title:** Electronics + embedded systems
  **description:** Gain hands-on experience with Arduino-style microcontrollers, sensors, wiring, and test plans for reliable data and control loops.

- **title:** Engineering design philosophy
  **description:** Practice iterative design, failure analysis, design reviews, and tradeoff thinking so your work improves every cycle.

## Section 2: Academic and STEM growth
**heading:** Academic and STEM growth

**items:**
- Apply math and physics to real systems: force balance, drag, stability margins, trajectories, and system-level constraints.
- Strengthen core STEM communication skills through documentation, presentations, and design justification.
- Work in a collaborative engineering environment where planning, testing, and iteration matter as much as raw ideas.

## Section 3: Preparation for collegiate rocketry
**heading:** Preparation for collegiate rocketry

**content:** The ETHS Rocketry workflow mirrors what collegiate teams look for: systems thinking, technical depth, strong collaboration, and evidence-based decisions. By graduation, members can point to authentic project work in CAD, simulation, electronics, software, and test operations — exactly the kind of experience that helps students contribute quickly on university rocketry teams.
